          	I announced my arrival as I entered this life,
          	A lavender door contrasted by iron streaked bricks. 
          	A dancer in mime's clothing, over time I learned
          	to raise my hand, my voice, and the spirits of those around me. 

          	As an artist might draw upon that sleeping dog on the porch, 
          	Or a musician on the unbridled joy captured in a child's twirl, 
          	I revel in the world I find myself perpetually lost in. 

          	But when it comes time for me to bid farewell,
          	May I leave as a soft sigh etched into 
          	the barren trees and the coursing river and the song of a robin,
          	quietly observing the passage of time.
